About PROJECT 13

PROJECT 13 is a hardware innovation hackathon designed to encourage students to explore the world of embedded systems and Arduino-based technology. The event provides a platform for students to transform creative ideas into working hardware prototypes while developing technical and problem-solving skills.

Participants will begin with an introductory Arduino workshop where they will gain knowledge about sensors, microcontrollers, and programming. After the workshop, teams will complete screening tasks designed to test their creativity and technical abilities.

The best teams will then be shortlisted to participate in the final hackathon round, where they will design, develop, and present innovative hardware solutions.

PROJECT 13 aims to promote creativity, collaboration, and engineering innovation among students.

FAQ

Who can participate in the event?

Students from any college or university who are interested in electronics and innovation can participate.

Do participants need prior Arduino knowledge?

No prior experience is required. The workshop will cover the basics.

What should participants bring, and will hardware be provided ?

Participants must bring at least one laptop per team for programming and development. Arduino kits and required hardware components for the final hackathon will be provided by the organizers.

What are the judging criteria for the projects ?

Projects will be evaluated based on innovation, technical implementation, functionality, and the effectiveness of the solution presented by the team.

Will certificates be given ?

Yes, participation certificates will be provided to all registered participants.
